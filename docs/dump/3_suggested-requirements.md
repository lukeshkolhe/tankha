1. Payroll, Taxes, Payslips & FnF (Q1, Q7, Q9): The core objective is "salary management" to help HR understand compensation data. Granular operational features like tax/deduction calculations, payslip generation, and Full & Final (FnF) settlements fall outside the core scope of an MVP. We highly recommend deliberately leaving these out to keep your project focused.
2. Querying Approach (Q2): The requirement to "answer questions about how the org pays people" is intentionally open-ended. Whether you choose to build structured dashboards/reports or an AI assistant is entirely up to your product judgment.
3. Users & Roles (Q3): Focus solely on the HR Manager persona provided in the prompt. Employee self-service and complex role-based access controls are not required.
4. Data Model, Components & History (Q5, Q6, Q8): You have full ownership over the employee schema. Decide whether tracking historical salary changes, building configurable salary components, and logging appraisal histories are necessary to meet the core goals.
5. Excel Workflows & Exports (Q4, Q10): Keep in mind the user is transitioning from an Excel-heavy process. You must decide whether building bulk import/export functionality provides the most value for your MVP, or if focusing strictly on managing the seeded 10,000-employee dataset through the UI is sufficient.
